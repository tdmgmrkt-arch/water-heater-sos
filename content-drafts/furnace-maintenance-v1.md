## 10-Point Pre-Winter Tune-Up Checklist

**H2:** Our 10-Point Pre-Winter Furnace Tune-Up

**Section intro (1 sentence):**
Every furnace maintenance visit with our HVAC team follows this 10-point checklist — no shortcuts, no items skipped because they take longer.

---

**Item 1 — Heat Exchanger Inspection**
We visually inspect the heat exchanger for cracks, holes, or corrosion. A cracked heat exchanger is the most serious furnace safety issue — it allows carbon monoxide to enter your home's air supply. Finding a crack in October is far better than finding it in January when the furnace has been running for weeks.

---

**Item 2 — Combustion Safety Test and CO Check**
We measure carbon monoxide levels in the flue and at the registers. If CO is detected in the circulating air, that's an immediate safety finding. Every maintenance visit ends with a confirmed combustion safety clearance — or a clear explanation of what needs to be fixed before the furnace runs this season.

---

**Item 3 — Burner Cleaning and Inspection**
We remove and inspect the burners for rust, scale, or debris. Dirty burners produce incomplete combustion, yellow flames, and higher gas consumption. Cleaned burners burn hotter and more efficiently.

---

**Item 4 — Flame Sensor Cleaning**
The flame sensor is the most common reason furnaces cycle on and briefly off. We clean the sensor rod to remove oxidation so it accurately signals to the control board that the burner lit. A clean flame sensor prevents nuisance shutdowns during heating season.

---

**Item 5 — Ignitor Test**
We test the hot-surface ignitor for proper resistance (measured in ohms) and visible integrity. Ignitors crack without warning — a cracked ignitor means a no-heat call in the middle of a cold night. We'll tell you if yours is getting marginal so you can decide whether to replace it proactively.

---

**Item 6 — Filter Inspection and Replacement**
We check the air filter and replace it if needed (standard 1-inch filters included in the tune-up price; media filters billed at cost). A clean filter is the single highest-return maintenance item on a furnace — it protects the blower motor, keeps the heat exchanger from overheating, and maintains airflow through the system.

---

**Item 7 — Blower Motor and Belt Inspection**
We inspect the blower motor for wear, measure amperage draw, and check belt tension on belt-drive systems. A blower motor drawing high amperage is close to failure. Catching it during maintenance means scheduling the repair on your terms — not as an emergency call.

---

**Item 8 — Gas Pressure Check**
We measure supply gas pressure and manifold pressure to verify the furnace is receiving the correct fuel input. Low gas pressure causes incomplete combustion and efficiency loss; high pressure can damage the heat exchanger over time.

---

**Item 9 — Electrical and Safety Control Check**
We test the limit switch, pressure switches, and control board for proper operation. The limit switch is the safety device that shuts the furnace off if the heat exchanger overheats — if it's faulty, the furnace either won't run or will run dangerously hot.

---

**Item 10 — Flue and Venting Inspection**
We inspect the flue pipe and venting connections for corrosion, blockages, or disconnects. A blocked or leaking flue can back-vent combustion gases — including CO — into the home. High-efficiency 90%+ furnaces with PVC condensate venting also get condensate trap and drain line inspection.

## Why Furnace Maintenance Matters

**H2:** Why Annual Maintenance Is Worth the Investment

**Section intro (1 sentence):**
A furnace maintenance visit does three things: it makes the system safer, more efficient, and less likely to break down at the worst time.

**Safety — the reason that outweighs all others:**
Furnaces are gas combustion appliances. When components wear out or become contaminated, the risk is not just a cold house — it's CO poisoning. HVAC maintenance plans that include a combustion safety test and CO check are the primary defense against this risk for households with gas heating. This is why the EPA and CPSC both recommend annual professional furnace inspections for gas appliances.

**Efficiency — what you can see on your utility bill:**
A dirty flame sensor, a blocked filter, or corroded burners all reduce combustion efficiency. The furnace runs longer cycles to reach the same temperature setpoint, burning more gas per unit of heat delivered. A clean, well-tuned furnace runs closer to its rated AFUE. On a 90% AFUE system, even a few percentage points of efficiency loss over the season adds up on the SoCalGas bill.

**Preventing breakdowns — the financial case:**
The average emergency furnace repair call in the Inland Empire costs $150–$600. The average tune-up costs $89–$199. An ignitor that's measured as marginal during a fall tune-up costs $150–$400 to replace proactively. That same ignitor failing on a January night costs a service call fee on top of the part. Maintenance doesn't eliminate all repairs — but it prevents the most predictable ones.

## FAQ

**H2:** Frequently Asked Questions About Furnace Maintenance

---

**Q1: How much does a furnace tune-up cost?**
A standard furnace tune-up in the Inland Empire costs $89–$199. The lower end covers a 10-point inspection with a standard filter. The higher end includes a media filter replacement and a more comprehensive duct and combustion analysis. Annual HVAC maintenance plans that bundle the furnace and AC tune-up typically run $150–$300 per year — we cover those pricing details on the [HVAC Maintenance Plan](/hvac-maintenance-plan) page.

---

**Q2: How often should a furnace be serviced?**
A gas furnace should be professionally serviced once per year, ideally in October or early November before the Inland Empire's heating season starts. Annual servicing keeps the system running at its rated efficiency, catches safety issues like a developing heat exchanger crack before the season begins, and satisfies most manufacturer warranty requirements for documented maintenance.

---

**Q3: What is included in a furnace tune-up?**
Our furnace tune-up includes a 10-point inspection: heat exchanger visual inspection, combustion safety and CO test, burner cleaning, flame sensor cleaning, ignitor resistance test, filter inspection and replacement, blower motor amperage check, gas pressure measurement, safety control testing (limit switch, pressure switches, control board), and flue and venting inspection. You receive a written summary of findings at the end of every visit.

---

**Q4: Why is CO safety part of a furnace tune-up?**
Gas furnaces produce carbon monoxide as a combustion byproduct. Under normal operation, CO exits through the flue. A cracked heat exchanger, a blocked flue, or a gas pressure problem can cause CO to enter the home's air supply instead. CO is colorless and odorless — it cannot be detected without a meter. A professional tune-up that includes a combustion safety test and CO measurement is the most reliable way to verify your furnace is operating safely.

---

**Q5: Do I need a furnace tune-up if my furnace is running fine?**
Yes. Most furnace problems develop gradually — a heat exchanger crack starts small, an ignitor degrades slowly, a flame sensor builds up oxidation over months. By the time the system shows obvious symptoms, the failure is imminent. Annual maintenance catches these issues in the early stage when they're less expensive to address and before they become a mid-winter emergency.

---

**Q6: When is the best time to schedule a furnace tune-up in the Inland Empire?**
October is the ideal window for Inland Empire homeowners. The heating season typically starts in November, and scheduling in October gives you enough lead time to address any findings before the first cold nights hit. November appointments are still available but book fast — the first cold week of the year floods every HVAC company's schedule. Scheduling in September or early October guarantees a pre-season slot.

---

**Q7: Does a furnace tune-up help with energy bills?**
Yes. A dirty flame sensor, blocked air filter, or degraded burners all reduce the furnace's combustion efficiency — the system runs longer to achieve the same heat output, burning more gas per cycle. A well-maintained furnace operates closer to its rated AFUE. On a 90%+ AFUE system, maintaining peak efficiency is especially important since the efficiency advantage over older systems depends on the combustion components staying clean and calibrated.

---

**Q8: Is a furnace maintenance plan worth it?**
For most Inland Empire homeowners, yes. An annual HVAC maintenance plan that covers both the furnace and AC tune-up typically costs $150–$300 per year — less than the average single repair call. Plans also include priority scheduling, which becomes a real advantage during the first cold week of the year when appointment availability tightens. See our [HVAC Maintenance Plan](/hvac-maintenance-plan) page for current plan options and pricing.
