## Our Approach

**H2:** How We Handle HVAC in the Inland Empire

The Inland Empire is not a typical California market. Riverside County and San Bernardino County sit in a bowl geography that traps heat — summer temperatures in Moreno Valley, Fontana, and Redlands regularly exceed 100°F and can spike past 110°F during heat domes. Those same areas can drop into the low 30s on January nights. Your HVAC system does more work here than almost anywhere else in California.

Our technicians are trained on the full range of residential HVAC equipment — central air systems, ductless mini-splits, gas furnaces, heat pump systems, and the refrigerants that power them (R-410A and the newer R-454B being phased in under the EPA's AIM Act). We hold the California C-20 (Warm-Air Heating, Ventilating and Air-Conditioning) license required by the CSLB for HVAC work in this state.

We also understand that the IE housing stock spans everything from 1960s tract homes in San Bernardino with aging ductwork to new construction in Menifee and Eastvale with modern zoning demands. Our assessment process accounts for that range — we size equipment correctly, we pull permits when required, and we don't cut corners to close a sale faster.

If you have a [heat pump water heater](/heat-pump-water-heaters) at home, you may also be a strong candidate for a [heat pump HVAC system](/heating-services) — both use the same refrigerant technology and can be integrated into a highly efficient whole-home setup. It's one of the most impactful upgrades available to IE homeowners right now.

**Q2: Are you a licensed HVAC contractor in California?**
Yes. We hold California Contractor License #1140776 under the C-20 (Warm-Air Heating, Ventilating and Air-Conditioning) classification required by the California State License Board (CSLB). Our technicians are also EPA 608 certified for refrigerant handling, as required by federal law for anyone servicing systems containing R-410A, R-454B, or legacy R-22 refrigerants.

**Q3: Do you offer 24/7 emergency HVAC service?**
Yes. We provide 24/7 emergency service for both heating and cooling failures across the Inland Empire. Call (800) 697-4014 any time — day or night, weekday or weekend. For true emergencies involving elderly household members, young children, or indoor temperatures above 105°F, tell us when you call and we prioritize your dispatch accordingly.

**Q4: What areas in the Inland Empire do you serve?**
We serve all of Riverside County and San Bernardino County — including Riverside, Moreno Valley, Corona, San Bernardino, Murrieta, Temecula, Rancho Cucamonga, Fontana, Ontario, Redlands, and dozens of surrounding cities. **Q5: Do you handle both AC and heating systems?**
Yes. We service, repair, and install both cooling and heating systems. This includes central air conditioners, ductless mini-splits, gas furnaces, heat pump systems, and thermostats. Having one licensed contractor handle both sides of your HVAC system simplifies scheduling, warranty tracking, and annual maintenance.

---

**Q6: Do you work on heat pump systems?**
Yes. We install and service heat pump HVAC systems — air-source heat pumps, ductless heat pump mini-splits, and whole-home heat pump systems. Heat pumps are increasingly popular in the Inland Empire because they provide both heating and cooling efficiently, and California's utility rebates and the federal IRA tax credit (up to $2,000) make them financially attractive. If you already have a [heat pump water heater](/heat-pump-water-heaters) in your home, a heat pump HVAC system pairs naturally since both use the same refrigerant technology.
