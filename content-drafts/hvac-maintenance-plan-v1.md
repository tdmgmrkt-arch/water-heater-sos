## Why Maintenance Matters

**H2:** Why Annual HVAC Maintenance Matters in the Inland Empire

Skipping maintenance doesn't mean nothing happens — it means the small problems don't get caught. A dirty evaporator coil forces the compressor to work harder. A worn capacitor that's still functional today is a failed capacitor in August. Low refrigerant that isn't caught in spring becomes an emergency in a 105-degree heat wave.

According to ENERGY STAR, a well-maintained central air conditioning system is significantly more efficient than one that hasn't been serviced — efficiency loss of 5–10% per neglected season compounds quickly over a system's 10–15 year lifespan. For Inland Empire homeowners running their AC from April through October, that efficiency gap shows up directly on the SCE bill.

Annual maintenance also protects manufacturer warranties. Most HVAC equipment manufacturers require documented annual servicing to keep warranty coverage valid. A tune-up receipt is cheap insurance against a voided warranty on a $6,000 system.
