## Installation Considerations

**H2:** What Goes Into a New AC Installation

**Section intro (1 sentence):**
Every Inland Empire home is different — here are the six factors that shape the right system and the right price for yours.

---

**Card 1**
**Title:** Load Calculation (Manual J)
**Description:** The size of the AC system you need is determined by a Manual J load calculation — not by your old unit's tonnage or a contractor's best guess. Factors include your home's square footage, ceiling height, insulation quality, window area, and sun exposure. An undersized unit runs constantly and never fully cools. An oversized unit short-cycles, spikes humidity, and wears out faster. We run the numbers before recommending any equipment.

---

**Card 2**
**Title:** SEER2 Efficiency Rating
**Description:** California requires all new residential AC systems to meet a minimum SEER2 14.3 efficiency rating — upgraded from the prior SEER 14 standard in 2023. Higher-efficiency systems (SEER2 18 or above) cost more upfront but reduce monthly cooling bills meaningfully over Inland Empire summers, which often run from May through October. We install systems across the efficiency range and will show you the payback math on any high-efficiency option.

---

**Card 3**
**Title:** Duct Condition Assessment
**Description:** A new system installed on leaking or undersized ductwork won't perform as designed. Before any installation, we inspect the existing duct system for leaks, blockages, and sizing adequacy. In older Riverside and San Bernardino homes, it's common to find duct losses of 20–30% — meaning nearly a third of your cooled air never reaches the living space. We'll let you know if duct work is needed and quote it separately so you can make an informed decision.

---

**Card 4**
**Title:** Equipment Brand Selection
**Description:** We install systems from Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, and other major manufacturers. Each brand offers a range of efficiency tiers and warranty terms. For most Inland Empire homes, a Carrier or Trane mid-efficiency system hits the best balance of reliability, parts availability, and manufacturer warranty coverage. We'll walk you through the options — we don't favor one brand for margin reasons.

---

**Card 5**
**Title:** Permits and Inspections
**Description:** AC installation in California requires a mechanical permit and city or county inspection in virtually every jurisdiction. We pull the permit, schedule the inspection, and handle the inspection process. Skipping a permit to save time is a risk that can void your homeowner's insurance coverage and create issues when you sell the home. Every installation we do is done by the book.

---

**Card 6**
**Title:** Financing Options
**Description:** At $4,500–$12,000 for a full system installation, most homeowners don't pay cash. We offer financing options to make the investment manageable. Ask about current terms when you call — we'll explain the monthly payment estimates for any system we quote so you can factor financing into your decision from day one.

## Cost

**H2:** How Much Does AC Installation Cost?

**AC installation in the Inland Empire typically costs between $4,500 and $12,000 for a complete system replacement, including the equipment and labor. Most standard single-zone central AC replacements fall in the $4,500–$7,500 range; larger homes, high-efficiency upgrades, or systems requiring duct modifications push toward the higher end.**

Here's how the cost range breaks down:

- **Standard efficiency (SEER2 14.3–16) — 2–3 ton system:** $4,500–$6,500
- **Mid-efficiency (SEER2 16–18) — 2–3 ton system:** $5,500–$8,000
- **High-efficiency (SEER2 18+) — 2–3 ton system:** $7,000–$10,000
- **Larger home or 4–5 ton system (mid-efficiency):** $7,500–$12,000
- **Duct modification or partial reroute (if needed):** $500–$2,500 additional
- **Permit fee (varies by city/county):** $150–$400

These are installed cost ranges — equipment plus labor. Cost varies by system brand, efficiency tier, local permit fees, and site conditions. We provide an exact quote after the in-home assessment.

**Financing note:** We offer financing options for qualified customers. Monthly payments on a $6,000 installation at common financing terms are in the range of $100–$150/month. Ask when you call.

**Replacement Decision Framing:**

**Replace your AC when repair costs exceed 50% of a new system's price and your unit is 10 or more years old, when refrigerant is R-22 (phased out and expensive to source), or when SEER ratings are below current California minimums. According to ENERGY STAR, central AC systems have a useful lifespan of 10–15 years — a 12-year-old unit that needs a $2,000 compressor repair is almost always a better replacement candidate than a repair investment.**

If you're not sure whether you need repair or full replacement, our technicians will give you an honest comparison. If repair makes more sense, we'll do the repair. If replacement makes more sense, we'll show you the numbers on a new system. We also offer [ductless mini-split installation](/mini-split-installation) for homes without ductwork or for zone-specific cooling needs. Need a quick fix while you plan a replacement? See our [AC repair](/ac-repair) page.

## FAQ

**H2:** Frequently Asked Questions About AC Installation

---

**Q1: How much does AC installation cost in the Inland Empire?**
A complete AC installation in the Inland Empire typically costs between $4,500 and $12,000, including equipment and labor. Most standard central AC replacements for a 3-bedroom home fall in the $4,500–$7,500 range. High-efficiency systems (SEER2 18+), larger homes, or jobs requiring duct modifications push the cost higher. We provide an exact itemized quote after a free in-home assessment — no obligation.

---

**Q2: How long does AC installation take?**
Most standard residential AC replacements are completed in one day. Our crew typically arrives in the morning, removes the old equipment, installs and commissions the new system, and tests the full cooling cycle before leaving. Larger systems, homes with duct work modifications, or multi-zone ductless installations may require two days. We'll give you a realistic timeline when we quote the job.

---

**Q3: What size AC do I need?**
The correct size depends on your home's square footage, insulation quality, ceiling height, sun exposure, and window area — not just square footage alone. We run a Manual J load calculation on every installation to spec the right tonnage. A 2,000 sq ft Inland Empire home typically needs a 3-ton system, but homes with poor insulation, high sun exposure, or open floor plans may need more. Oversizing is a common mistake — an oversized unit short-cycles and creates humidity problems.

---

**Q4: Do I need a permit for AC installation in California?**
Yes. AC installation requires a mechanical permit in virtually every California city and county. We pull the permit, coordinate the inspection, and manage the process from start to finish. Never let a contractor skip the permit — it creates problems with homeowner's insurance and can be a serious obstacle when selling the home.

---

**Q5: What brands do you install?**
We install systems from Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, and other major manufacturers. We'll walk you through the options for your budget and home during the in-home assessment. Most Inland Empire homeowners end up choosing a Carrier or Trane system for the combination of reliability, parts availability, and warranty terms — but we install what fits your needs, not what earns us the best margin.

---

**Q6: What is SEER2 and does it matter?**
SEER2 (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio 2) is the current efficiency rating standard for residential AC systems. California requires all new residential systems to meet a minimum SEER2 14.3 rating. Higher-rated systems (SEER2 18+) use less electricity per unit of cooling, which matters in the Inland Empire where cooling season runs May through October and summer utility bills are a real concern. We'll show you the estimated annual savings on any high-efficiency upgrade we quote.

---

**Q7: Do you offer financing for AC installation?**
Yes. We offer financing options for qualified customers. For a $6,000 installation, monthly payments at common financing terms typically fall in the $100–$150/month range. If upfront cost is a factor in your decision, ask about financing when you call or when we do your in-home assessment — we'll structure the quote around your budget.

---

**Q8: Should I repair or replace my AC?**
Replace when repair costs exceed 50% of a new system's cost AND your unit is 10 or more years old — or when the system uses R-22 refrigerant (phased out and costly to source). Repair when the unit is newer and the repair is minor. Per ENERGY STAR, central AC systems have an average lifespan of 10–15 years, so a 12-year-old unit needing a major repair is almost always a replacement candidate. We give you both quotes and let you decide.
