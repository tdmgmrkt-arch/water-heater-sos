## AC Maintenance Checklist

**H2:** Our 10-Point AC Maintenance Checklist

**Section intro (1 sentence):**
Every tune-up we perform covers these ten checkpoints — no items skipped, no upsells unless something is actually worn or failing.

---

**Item 1**
**Title:** Air Filter Inspection and Replacement
**Description:** We inspect the existing filter and replace it if dirty or degraded. A clogged filter is the single most common cause of reduced AC efficiency and frozen coils in Inland Empire homes, where dust and airborne particulate levels are higher than coastal CA averages. We recommend checking your filter monthly during peak cooling season.

---

**Item 2**
**Title:** Evaporator Coil Cleaning
**Description:** The evaporator coil absorbs heat from the air inside your home. A dirty coil can't absorb heat efficiently, causing the system to run longer and cool less. We inspect and clean the coil during every maintenance visit. Coil cleaning alone can recover 5–10% of lost efficiency in a system that hasn't been serviced in more than a year.

---

**Item 3**
**Title:** Condenser Coil Inspection and Cleaning
**Description:** The condenser coil (outside unit) releases heat to the outdoor air. Dirt, cottonwood, and debris on the condenser coil reduce its ability to dissipate heat, forcing the compressor to work harder in already-hot Inland Empire conditions. We clean the condenser fins and check for physical damage.

---

**Item 4**
**Title:** Refrigerant Level Check
**Description:** Low refrigerant is a symptom — it means there's a leak somewhere in the system. During every tune-up, we check system pressure to confirm refrigerant is at the correct charge level. If levels are low, we note it and offer a leak diagnostic separately. We don't top off refrigerant without addressing the source of the leak.

---

**Item 5**
**Title:** Electrical Components Inspection
**Description:** We test capacitors, contactors, and electrical connections for wear, corrosion, and out-of-spec readings. Capacitor failure is the most common AC breakdown in the Inland Empire — extreme summer heat degrades them faster than in milder climates. Catching a weak capacitor during maintenance ($150–$350 replacement) prevents a no-cooling emergency call in July.

---

**Item 6**
**Title:** Blower Motor and Fan Inspection
**Description:** We inspect the blower motor, fan blades, and motor bearings for wear and proper operation. A failing blower reduces airflow throughout the system, creating hot spots in the home and putting stress on the compressor. We lubricate motor bearings where applicable and note any components showing early failure signs.

---

**Item 7**
**Title:** Condensate Drain Line Flush
**Description:** The condensate drain line removes humidity from the air. It clogs with algae and debris over time — and when it does, it shuts the system off or leaks water into ceilings and walls. We flush the drain line during every maintenance visit with a cleaning solution and test the float switch.

---

**Item 8**
**Title:** Thermostat Calibration Check
**Description:** We verify the thermostat is reading accurately and communicating correctly with the air handler and outdoor unit. A thermostat that reads 2°F high or low will cause the system to run inefficiently. If you have an older thermostat, we'll note whether a smart thermostat upgrade would improve system control and efficiency.

---

**Item 9**
**Title:** Airflow and Duct Spot-Check
**Description:** We measure airflow from supply registers in multiple rooms and check for obvious duct leaks at accessible connections. Significant airflow imbalance or pressure loss can indicate duct issues that deserve a full duct assessment. We flag these rather than oversell a duct service — but we don't ignore the signals either.

---

**Item 10**
**Title:** System Performance Test
**Description:** We run the system through a full cooling cycle and verify the temperature differential between supply and return air (the Delta-T). A properly functioning residential AC system should produce a 16–22°F temperature drop across the air handler. If the Delta-T is outside that range, we diagnose why before leaving.

## FAQ

**H2:** Frequently Asked Questions About AC Maintenance

---

**Q1: How much does an AC tune-up cost?**
A standard AC tune-up in the Inland Empire runs $89–$199. Most single-system residential tune-ups fall in the $89–$149 range. If the tune-up reveals a part that needs replacement — a worn capacitor, for example — we quote that separately before doing anything. The tune-up fee covers the inspection and 10-point service regardless of what's found.

---

**Q2: How often should I service my AC?**
At minimum, once per year — ideally in the spring before Inland Empire temperatures start climbing. Twice per year (spring and fall) is the optimal schedule: spring service prepares the cooling system for summer, fall service prepares the heating system for winter. Our annual maintenance plan covers both visits.

---

**Q3: What does AC maintenance include?**
Our 10-point tune-up includes: air filter inspection and replacement, evaporator coil inspection and cleaning, condenser coil cleaning, refrigerant level check, electrical component testing (capacitors, contactors), blower motor inspection, condensate drain flush, thermostat calibration check, airflow spot-check, and a full system performance test. We document every item and walk you through results before leaving.

---

**Q4: Does regular AC maintenance save money?**
Yes. A well-maintained AC runs up to 15% more efficiently than a neglected system (ENERGY STAR). During peak cooling season in the Inland Empire, that efficiency difference shows up on your monthly utility bill. More importantly, tune-ups catch the components most likely to fail — capacitors, condensate drains, low refrigerant — when they're still cheap to address, instead of during a heat wave when an emergency call costs more and takes longer.

---

**Q5: Does AC maintenance extend system lifespan?**
Yes. ENERGY STAR reports that the average central AC system has a lifespan of 10–15 years. Regular maintenance keeps systems toward the 15-year end of that range. Skipping maintenance causes parts to wear faster, efficiency to drop, and systems to fail earlier — typically requiring replacement 2–4 years sooner than a well-maintained unit.

---

**Q6: Will maintenance void my warranty if I don't use the manufacturer's technician?**
No — you can use any licensed HVAC contractor for maintenance without voiding your warranty under federal Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act protections. What does matter is that service is documented and performed annually. We provide written service records you can use for any warranty claim.

---

**Q7: Do you offer annual maintenance plans?**
Yes. Our HVAC maintenance plan covers two visits per year (spring AC tune-up + fall furnace or heat pump tune-up), priority scheduling, and plan member discounts on any repairs found during service visits. Details and current pricing are at [/hvac-maintenance-plan](/hvac-maintenance-plan) (forthcoming). Ask about plan options when you call.

---

**Q8: What's the best time to schedule AC maintenance in the Inland Empire?**
March through April is ideal — before the first heat wave and before pre-summer slots fill up. May appointments are still available early in the month. By late May and June, demand increases significantly and scheduling gets tight. If you're reading this in summer, book now for next spring or call to ask about current availability — we sometimes have morning slots even in peak season.
